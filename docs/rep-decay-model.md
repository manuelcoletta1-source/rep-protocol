# REP Decay Model

The Reconstructible Event Protocol (REP) defines conditions under which an event
ceases to remain reconstructible.

An event decays when one or more of the required operational conditions can no
longer be reconstructed.

The four required conditions are:

Decision  
Cost  
Trace  
Time  

## Decay Conditions

An Event Unit decays if any of the following occurs:

- the decision attribution becomes unreadable or unverifiable
- the cost cannot be localized
- the trace cannot be reconstructed
- the temporal exposure becomes indeterminate

## Operational Consequence

When decay occurs, the event becomes non-reconstructible within the REP domain.

This does not imply that the event did not occur.  
It only means that its operational reconstruction is no longer possible.

## Historical Persistence

REP does not require deletion of decayed events.

Historical records may remain stored in registries, but they lose
operational validity if reconstructibility conditions are no longer met.
